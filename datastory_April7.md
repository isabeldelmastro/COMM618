# [Opiod deaths are plummeting. Here’s what worked.](https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/2026/04/06/opioid-overdose-deaths-drug-supply-addiction/)



This story covers the recently released statistics by the CDC about opioid overdose deaths, and over deaths dropping from 2024 to 2025. Sam Quinones, a Washington Post journalist who has written many books about opioid addiction, uses the article as a platform to theorize what contributed to the drop in overdose deaths nationwide. The most "important" reason Quinones cites is Fentanyl supplies dropping because of counternarcotics strategies. 

To be honest, I don’t know how I feel about opinion pieces that take a recently released statistic and theorize causation. The article and Quinones’ credits clearly demonstrate that he is an expert in opioid addiction, but I would much rather prefer to read a well rounded article where Quinones quotes multiple expert opinions and cites reputable data instead of an opinion piece.
