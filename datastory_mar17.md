**[How a Melting Glacier Could Affect Tens of Millions Around the Globe](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2026/03/17/climate/thwaites-glacier-rising-sea-level-risk-floods.html)**

This data story reports on how cities around the world could be affected if the Thwaites Glacier breaks apart. More specifically, author Mira Rojanasakul reports on how low lying areas in Asian cities (Bangkok, Shanghai, Ho Chi Minh City, etc) are at at high rish of threats from the possibility of Thwaites Glacier breaking.

The story is accompanied with maps of that highlight the areas within and outside at risk cities. It also has a graphs that show the number of people in Dhaka, New Orleans, Stockton, and Miami who live within two feet of high tide. Those that live in the two feet zone are at threatened by Thwaites Glacier breaking.

I wish the article discussed the actual risks of Thwaites Glaicer breaking more extensively. The article references Thwaites "threating" cities, or describes cities as "at risk," but they never mention what the actual risks are.

Furthermore, many of the graphs that show how many people live within two feet of high tide do not show enough points on the x-axis. Some graphs fail to show any numbers at all. It's hard to understand the numerical context of people at risk to the effects of Thwaites Glacier breaking when the other bars in the bar chart are given no numerical values.
