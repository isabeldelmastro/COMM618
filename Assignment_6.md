This is data from the United States Geological Survey: a bureau within the U.S. Department of the Interior that functions as a biological science agency. that documents the number of wildfires in the United States from as far back as 1990. For the purposes of our analysis, we are focusing on the number of wildfires from 2014-2024.

From 2014 to 2024, the number of wildfires incidents in the United States has fluctuated significantly. In 2017 the number of wildfires in United States saw a record high with over 71 thousand incidents. Shortly thereafter, the United States experienced a sudden drop in wildfires over the next two years. from 2017 to 2019, the number of wildfire incidents in the United States dropped 34.47%. Over the next four years, the number of wildfires has fluctuated significantly, with the second highest number of wildfires occuring in 2022 with over 68 thousand incidents before dropping significantly the following year.
