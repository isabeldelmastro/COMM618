# [The Hair-Loss Drug Rewriting the Rules of Masculinity](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/07/magazine/male-hair-loss-treatment-finasteride.html)

The Hair-Loss Drug Rewriting the Rules of Masculinity by Susan Dominus explores the positives and negatives effects of finasteride on patients who want to reduce hair loss. The article cites statistics form the American Hair Loss Association, in with 85 percent of men will have significant hairloss by the age of 50, and two-thirds men will start to experience hair thining by their mid-thirties.

The article also references the spike of perscriptions for the drug form 2017 to 2024: they trippled over those 7 years. And the side effects, in which one in 20 men experience side effects form oral finasteride.

I think this is an very engaging article: The annecdotes keep the story engaging and easy to follow, but I wish there were more statistics presented about the changes of side effects in men over time, but I am not quite sure the data exists.
